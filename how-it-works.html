How it works

1. Install Mixture

Mixture it's a Mac/Win development framework that makes your life quite easy. Useful if you want to prototype stuff, or build a simple website. If you're familiar with Jekyll, you'll find it similar as it's also based on Liquid.

(I know, Mixture it's not free, but for Â£25 is quite handy)

 

2. Fork it on github

Or clone it, or download it. Whatever the right terminology is. Basically get the files in your computer, give the folder your favourite name, and open it using Mixture.

If you plan to use git, you know what you have to do better than me.

 

3. Add your styles

Do not touch

  • _boilerplate.scss

Do touch

Any other scss file. Basically do whatever you want, add custom styles on the style.scss, _guide.scss (this should where your live site styles are), _layout.scss (this is where your live site layout styles should be).

But this is just how I've organised my SCSS. Do whatever you want with your files. It's your site. Just remember to import the SCSS partials into style.scss.

 

4. Add your webfonts/javascript

If you don't need additional javascript (jQuery 1.x goes by default) or webfonts, move to the next point.

If you do need to add stuff, edit the file templates > includes > head.liquid.

 

5. Check your site looks fine

By now the site should look like quite like you intented. Unless something is broken. Which is quite likely as this is purely an experiment. If that happens, open an issue on github, or get in touch via Twitter.

 

6. Let your content shine

Now that everything works fine, it's time to add your content. You'll be mainly editing, adding and deleting files under the **collections** folder.

There are four folders (blocks, elements, layouts, strategy). As you can imagine, adding or deleting .liquid files will change the content of those categories. Navigation will be generated on the fly by Mixture.

So start editing files, creating new ones (just follow the Mixture documentation), and deleting what you don't need.

At some point you will like to explore the templates folder, where you could edit the homepage (index.liquid) and other stuff. Hopefully by now you won't need this guide anymore.

Have fun using this boilerplate! For comments, complaints or suggestions simply drop me a line.
